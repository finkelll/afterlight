# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** Finkel
**Date:** 2026-03-06

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Terminology for core concepts in Afterlight (rooms, threads, candles, and related terms)

**Goals:** Alternatives for all core terms (room, thread, candle) and supporting terms; shortlist of 2–3 naming options per concept; criteria to judge names (emotional fit, clarity, differentiation from chat rooms/threads); must-avoids.

### Context Guidance

From your PRD and UX spec, the product currently uses a **ritual-shaped model** with three main terms:

- **Room** — The memorial space “for” the person who died. The organizer creates it (name, description, photo, visibility, approval settings), shares an invite link, and moderates. Visitors land here and see who the room is for and the list of threads.
- **Thread** — Themed containers inside a room (e.g. “Her laugh,” “What she taught us,” “Stories we tell”). Each thread holds multiple candles; contributors choose a thread when they “light a candle.”
- **Candle** — A single contribution: text (required) plus optional photo/audio/video. The action is “Light a candle”; each candle shows who lit it and when.

The UX is built around this hierarchy (room → threads → candles) and emotional safety. Terminology affects wayfinding, copy, and how people describe the product.

### Session Setup

**Confirmed scope:** All terms (room, thread, candle) plus supporting terms (e.g. invite link, “Light a candle,” organizer, contributor).

**Confirmed outcomes:** Shortlist of 2–3 naming options per concept; criteria to judge names (emotional fit, clarity, differentiation from “chat rooms” / “threads”); explicit must-avoids.
