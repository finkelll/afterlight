# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** Finkel
**Date:** 2026-03-06

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Terminology for core concepts in Afterlight (rooms, threads, candles, and related terms)

**Goals:** Alternatives for all core terms (room, thread, candle) and supporting terms; shortlist of 2–3 naming options per concept; criteria to judge names (emotional fit, clarity, differentiation from chat rooms/threads); must-avoids.

### Context Guidance

From your PRD and UX spec, the product currently uses a **ritual-shaped model** with three main terms:

- **Room** — The memorial space “for” the person who died. The organizer creates it (name, description, photo, visibility, approval settings), shares an invite link, and moderates. Visitors land here and see who the room is for and the list of threads.
- **Thread** — Themed containers inside a room (e.g. “Her laugh,” “What she taught us,” “Stories we tell”). Each thread holds multiple candles; contributors choose a thread when they “light a candle.”
- **Candle** — A single contribution: text (required) plus optional photo/audio/video. The action is “Light a candle”; each candle shows who lit it and when.

The UX is built around this hierarchy (room → threads → candles) and emotional safety. Terminology affects wayfinding, copy, and how people describe the product.

### Session Setup

**Confirmed scope:** All terms (room, thread, candle) plus supporting terms (e.g. invite link, “Light a candle,” organizer, contributor).

**Confirmed outcomes:** Shortlist of 2–3 naming options per concept; criteria to judge names (emotional fit, clarity, differentiation from “chat rooms” / “threads”); explicit must-avoids.

## Technique Selection

**Approach:** Progressive Technique Flow  
**Journey design:** Systematic development from broad naming ideas to shortlists, criteria, and must-avoids.

**Progressive techniques:**

- **Phase 1 – Expansive exploration:** Cross-Pollination — borrow naming patterns from other domains (memorials, rituals, spaces, apps) to generate many term alternatives for room, thread, candle, and supporting terms.
- **Phase 2 – Pattern recognition:** Mind Mapping — cluster and organize generated terms by concept and theme (ritual vs. neutral, spatial vs. abstract) to see structure and gaps.
- **Phase 3 – Idea development:** Trait Transfer — define traits we want names to have (emotional fit, clarity, differentiation) and refine to 2–3 strong options per concept.
- **Phase 4 – Action planning:** Solution Matrix — turn criteria and must-avoids into a decision framework and lock the shortlist (2–3 options per concept) plus a must-avoid list.

**Journey rationale:** Terminology benefits from wide divergence first (many options, multiple “families” of language), then structure (themes and patterns), then convergence (apply criteria to get 2–3 per concept), then a clear framework (criteria + must-avoids) for final choice and copy.

### Pattern summary

- **Main space:** Strong in ritual (sanctuary, shrine), memorial (memorial, tribute), spatial (room, home, place), and narrative (legacy, story, collection). “Room” sits in spatial; alternatives span ritual and calm-neutral.
- **Themed container:** Strong in narrative (chapter, section, story) and neutral (topic, theme, channel). “Thread” is neutral/forum; ritual options (intention, station) are fewer. Spatial (nook, alcove, wing) and nature (path, trail) offer distinct tone.
- **Single contribution:** Ritual (candle, light, offering) matches current product. Neutral (message, entry, contribution) and narrative (memory, story, moment) are alternatives if you want to soften ritual.
- **Gaps:** Themed container has no strong memorial-specific term; “theme” or “topic” are generic. Main space has many options; contribution has a clear ritual set (candle, light, offering) vs. non-ritual set.

### Implications for Phase 3

- **Differentiation:** Avoid generic forum/social terms (thread, post, channel, board) if you want to stay clear of “chat room” or “thread” associations.
- **Emotional fit:** Ritual and nature families support calm, meaning; neutral supports clarity and low metaphor.
- **Consistency:** Choosing one family across all three concepts (e.g. ritual: sanctuary → intention → candle) can feel coherent; mixing families (e.g. room + thread + candle) is also valid if intentional.

### User reactions (affinities and constraints)

- **Main space:** Like — **room**, **space**, **tribute**.
- **Themed container:** Dislike — **thread** (conflict with Threads by Facebook). Like — **memory**, **tribute**, **moment**.
- **Single contribution:** Like **candle** for the concept (the thing). For the action: prefer **light** (e.g. “Light” as CTA) over “light a candle” where it helps.

## Deep dive: Main Space

**Goal:** Find a main-space term that feels **lasting** (not temporary), fits “a shared home for memories,” and—if we move off “room”—clearly means “the whole memorial for this one person.”

### What “room” does well

- **Familiar and warm** — “A room for Mom” is intuitive; it’s an enclosed, intimate place.
- **Spatial** — Holds “threads” and “candles” without sounding techy.
- **Differentiated in context** — In a memorial product, “room” reads as memorial space, not chat.
- **Already in use** — No relearn for you or users.

### What might feel “not quite best”

- **Permanence** — “Room” can suggest a temporary or generic place (e.g. “chat room,” “meeting room”). If the doubt is “I want it to feel lasting,” we need a term that implies durability or ongoing presence.
- **Singularity** — It should be obvious this is *one* place for *one* person. “Room” does that; some alternatives do it more explicitly (e.g. “memorial,” “their place”).
- **Gravitas** — You might want something that feels a bit more weighty or dedicated than “room” without going religious.

### Alternatives that emphasize “lasting” or “home”

| Term | Lasting? | Warmth | Clarity | Differentiation | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|---------|------------------|--------|
| **Room** | Neutral | High | High | Good in context | Your current lean; familiar. |
| **Memorial** | High | Medium | High | Strong | Explicitly lasting; “their memorial.” Can feel formal. |
| **Place** | Medium | Medium | High | Good | “A place for [Person].” Neutral, flexible. |
| **Home** | High | High | High | Good | “A home for memories” matches product line. Risk: “home” can mean house or app home. |
| **Sanctuary** | High | High | Medium | Strong | Feels lasting and respectful; may read religious for some. |
| **Haven** | High | High | Medium | Strong | Safe, lasting; slightly literary. |
| **Garden** | High | High | Medium | Strong | Grows over time; not temporary. Can feel metaphorical. |
| **Remembrance** | High | High | Medium | Strong | “Their remembrance” = the whole space. Slightly abstract. |
| **Legacy** | High | Medium | Medium | Good | Lasting by definition; can feel formal or legacy-product. |
| **Shrine** | High | High | High | Strong | Very lasting/sacred; may be too religious for some. |

### Recommendation (main space)

- **If “room” still feels right after this:** Keep **room**. It’s clear, warm, and in your vision (“a shared home for memories”); the “home” is the product, the “room” is the person’s place inside it.
- **If you want more “lasting” in the word:** **Memorial** or **place** — “their memorial,” “a place for [Person].” Both read as enduring and non-temporary.
- **If you want more warmth + lasting without “room”:** **Home** (with clear product framing so “home” isn’t confused with app home) or **sanctuary** (if you’re okay with a slight ritual tone).

**Refined main-space shortlist:** **Room** · **Memorial** · **Place** · **Home** (use with clear context) · **Sanctuary**. Pick one; the rest can stay in the doc as alternatives if you A/B test copy later.

## Final terminology (locked)

**Main space:** **Room** — The whole memorial for one person. One room per person.

**Themed containers (two types):**

1. **Moment** — For sharing a memory or a moment with that person (e.g. “Her laugh,” “Stories we tell”). Content = memories/moments.
2. **Tribute** — For dedicating something to the person: e.g. a song, a flower, a candle (as a container), or a prayer. Content = dedications (media, ritual, or words framed as dedication).

Rooms can contain both Moments and Tributes; each is a container type with its own purpose.

**Single contribution:** **Candle** — One person’s contribution (text + optional photo/audio/video) inside a Moment or Tribute. Keep the noun “candle.”

**Primary action:** **Light** — Use “Light” as the main CTA (e.g. button). Longer copy: “Light a candle” or “Add your light” where it reads well.
